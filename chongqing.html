City Guide

Chongqing

China's mountain city of fire and flavour

Overview

The Mountain City

A sprawling metropolis carved into steep hillsides where the Yangtze and Jialing rivers meet.

Where History Meets Elevation

Chongqing is one of China's four direct-controlled municipalities and home to over 30 million people, making it one of the largest urban areas on Earth. Known as the "Mountain City" (山城), it is built across steep hills and dramatic cliffs at the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing rivers in southwest China.

During World War II, Chongqing served as China's wartime capital, and its resilience from that era still shapes the city's identity today. Now a major economic hub in western China, the city is a thrilling mix of old and new — from the towering stilted structure of Hongyadong, to the bustling Jiefangbei pedestrian square, to its world-famous multi-level urban landscape where roads, bridges, and buildings stack at wildly different elevations.

Weather & Nature

Fog, Furnace & Spectacular Scenery

A subtropical climate that defines the city's character, surrounded by UNESCO-listed natural wonders.

One of China's Three Furnaces

Chongqing is famous for its extreme summers — it is historically known as one of China's "Three Furnaces" (alongside Wuhan and Nanjing), with temperatures often exceeding 40 degrees Celsius from June to August. The humidity is relentless, and locals believe this fiery climate is precisely why Chongqing people love eating such intensely spicy food.

In winter, the city earns its other nickname: "Fog Capital" (雾都). Thick mist blankets the hills and river valleys, giving Chongqing an atmospheric, almost cinematic quality. The best time to visit is September to November, when the heat breaks, the air clears, and the autumn colours add warmth to the dramatic landscape.

  • Yangtze River and the legendary Three Gorges
  • Wulong Karst formations — a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Dazu Rock Carvings — over 50,000 Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian sculptures
  • Best visit window: September through November
Food & Cuisine

Birthplace of the Hotpot

Bold, spicy, and numbing — Chongqing's food scene is an unforgettable assault on the senses.

Fire on the Table

Chongqing is the undisputed birthplace of hotpot — the city's most iconic culinary tradition. The classic Chongqing hotpot features a bubbling cauldron of bright red chilli oil, packed with Sichuan peppercorns that deliver the famous "mala" (numbing-spicy) sensation. Locals gather around these pots nightly, dipping everything from tripe and duck blood to lotus root and mushrooms into the fiery broth.

But the food scene goes far beyond hotpot. Chongqing's street food culture is legendary, with dishes you will not find anywhere else in the world.

  • Chongqing Hotpot — spicy red oil broth, the city's signature dish
  • Xiaomian — fiery, oily noodles with chilli and peanuts, a breakfast staple
  • Suanla Fen — hot and sour sweet potato glass noodles
  • Wanzhou Grilled Fish — whole fish roasted with chillies and spices
  • Chuan Chuan Xiang — skewered meats and vegetables cooked in spicy broth
  • Malatang — pick-your-own-ingredients spicy soup with sesame dipping sauce
Things to Do

Experiences You Cannot Miss

From ancient towns to gravity-defying monorails, Chongqing is a city of extraordinary moments.

A City Like No Other

Chongqing rewards the curious traveller at every turn. This is a city where a monorail passes straight through the middle of an apartment building, where you can enter a building on the first floor and exit on the tenth — onto a different street — and where the night skyline rivals Hong Kong for sheer drama.

  • Visit Hongyadong at night — the 11-story stilted complex lit up like a scene from Spirited Away
  • Take a Yangtze River cruise through the legendary Three Gorges
  • Wander Ciqikou Ancient Town for traditional crafts, tea houses, and street snacks
  • Ride the Liziba Monorail that passes directly through a residential building
  • Watch the night skyline from Nanshan — the "One Tree Viewing Platform" (一棵树观景台)
  • Soak in natural hot springs in the surrounding mountainous countryside

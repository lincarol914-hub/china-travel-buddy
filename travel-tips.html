Everything you need to know about visas, entry requirements, and practical tips for a smooth trip to China.

Entry Requirements

China Visa Policies

China has recently expanded visa-free access for many countries. Here's what you need to know before you go.

📢

Latest Update: Expanded Visa-Free Access

As of 2024, China has introduced visa-free transit and entry policies for travellers from over 50 countries. Many European, Asian, and Oceanian passport holders can now enter China visa-free for stays of up to 15 or 30 days. Always check the latest policy before your trip as regulations may change.

Most Popular
🛂

Visa-Free Entry (15/30 Days)

Citizens from eligible countries can enter China without a visa for short stays.

  • Available for 50+ countries including UK, France, Germany, Australia, and more
  • Stay up to 15 or 30 days depending on nationality
  • Valid for tourism, business visits, family visits, and transit
  • No pre-application needed — just arrive with a valid passport
✈

Transit Visa-Free (72/144 Hours)

Travellers transiting through China can stay visa-free for up to 144 hours in select cities.

  • Available at major airports including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Chengdu
  • Must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third country
  • Stay limited to the designated transit region
  • Covers 54 eligible nationalities
📄

Tourist Visa (L Visa)

For travellers who need a standard visa or plan longer stays in China.

  • Apply at your local Chinese embassy or visa centre
  • Single, double, or multiple entry options available
  • Typically valid for 30–90 days per entry
  • Requires passport, application form, photo, and itinerary
🏝

Hainan Island (30 Days Visa-Free)

Special visa-free policy for Hainan Island, China's tropical paradise.

  • Available for citizens from 59 countries
  • Stay up to 30 days on Hainan Island
  • Must book through a registered travel agency
  • Cannot travel to mainland China without a separate visa
Before You Go

Essential Travel Tips

Practical advice to make your China trip as smooth as possible.

📱

Set Up Apps in Advance

Download WeChat and Alipay before you arrive. Link an international card for mobile payments — cash is rarely accepted in cities.

🌐

VPN & Internet Access

Google, Instagram, and WhatsApp are blocked in China. Download a reliable VPN before arriving, or use a China SIM with roaming data.

💳

Payment Methods

China is nearly cashless. Alipay and WeChat Pay are accepted almost everywhere. International cards work at hotels and airports, but rarely elsewhere.

🚇

Getting Around

China's high-speed rail network is world-class. Book train tickets on Trip.com or 12306. Metro systems in major cities are cheap and efficient.

📞

Local SIM Card

Buy a SIM card at the airport for data and calls. China Mobile and China Unicom offer tourist-friendly plans. Passport registration required.

⚡

Power & Adapters

China uses Type A, C, and I power sockets (220V). Bring a universal adapter. Most hotels provide USB charging ports.

Checklist

Pre-Trip Checklist

Make sure you've ticked these off before boarding your flight to China.

1

Check Visa Requirements

Confirm whether you qualify for visa-free entry or need to apply for a visa at your local Chinese embassy.

2

Download Essential Apps

WeChat, Alipay, Didi (ride-hailing), Baidu Maps, and a translation app like Google Translate (download offline pack).

3

Set Up Mobile Payments

Link your international bank card to Alipay or WeChat Pay. Test it before departure to avoid issues on arrival.

4

Prepare a VPN

Install and configure a VPN app before entering China. It won't be downloadable once you're there.

5

Carry Passport Copies

Keep digital and printed copies of your passport, visa, and travel insurance. Hotels require passport check-in.

6

Book Accommodation Early

Not all hotels accept foreign guests. Use Trip.com or Booking.com and confirm foreigner-friendly status before booking.

China Visa Tips

Everything you need to know about getting your visa sorted before travelling to China.

💡

Important: Always Check the Latest Policy

China's visa policies can change frequently. The information below is a general guide — always verify with the nearest Chinese embassy or consulate, or check the official website for the most up-to-date requirements before you apply.

📋

Common Visa Types for Tourists

Most travellers to China need an L (Tourist) visa. Here are the main types relevant to visitors:

  • L Visa (Tourist) — For sightseeing, visiting family or friends. Single, double, or multiple entry options available.
  • M Visa (Business) — For commercial and trade activities. Required if you're attending trade fairs or business meetings.
  • S Visa (Private / Family Visit) — For visiting family members who are working or studying in China.
  • Transit Visa-Free — Many nationalities can transit through China visa-free for 72, 144, or 240 hours depending on the port of entry.
✈️

Transit Visa-Free Policy (TWOV)

China offers generous transit visa-free stays for many nationalities. This is perfect for short trips or stopovers.

240-Hour (10-Day) Visa-Free Transit: Available at major cities including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and more. You must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third country/region (different from where you arrived from).
  • Available to passport holders from 54+ countries including the UK, US, Canada, Australia, and most EU nations
  • You must enter and exit through designated ports in the same administrative region (or approved cross-region routes)
  • You cannot work or study during your transit stay
  • Always carry your onward flight ticket confirmation
📝

How to Apply for a China Visa

The application process is straightforward if you have the right documents ready.

  1. Complete the online visa application form at the Chinese Visa Application Service Centre (CVASC) website for your country
  2. Gather your supporting documents: valid passport (6+ months validity, blank pages), passport photo, flight booking, hotel reservations, and travel itinerary
  3. Book an appointment at your nearest Chinese Visa Application Centre or embassy
  4. Attend your appointment in person — submit your documents and pay the visa fee
  5. Collect your passport with the visa (usually within 4-7 working days, or pay extra for express processing)
📂

Documents You'll Need

Make sure you have these ready before your appointment to avoid delays:

  • Valid passport — Must have at least 6 months validity remaining and at least 2 blank visa pages
  • Completed application form — Filled out online, printed and signed
  • Passport-sized photo — Recent, white background, 48mm x 33mm (specific requirements vary by country)
  • Flight booking confirmation — Round-trip ticket or onward travel proof
  • Hotel reservations — For each city you plan to visit; if staying with friends/family, an invitation letter is needed
  • Travel itinerary — A day-by-day plan showing which cities you'll visit
  • Proof of financial means — Bank statements or employment letter (sometimes required)
  • Previous Chinese visas — If you've visited China before, bring old passports with previous visas
💰

Costs & Processing Times

Fees vary by nationality and visa type. Here are typical ranges:

  • UK citizens: £151 for single entry, £174 for double entry, £226 for multiple entry (6 months or 1 year)
  • US citizens: ~$185 for a 10-year multiple entry visa
  • Standard processing: 4-7 working days
  • Express processing: 2-3 working days (additional fee applies)
  • Rush processing: 1 working day (additional fee, subject to availability)
Tip: Apply at least 4-6 weeks before your trip. During peak season (Chinese New Year, National Day Golden Week), processing can take longer.
🌟

Top Tips for a Smooth Application

  • Apply early — Don't leave it to the last minute. Start the process at least 1 month before departure.
  • Be specific with your itinerary — Chinese visa offices want to see detailed plans. Include dates, cities, and accommodation for each day.
  • Book refundable flights/hotels — You need bookings for the application, but plans might change. Use refundable options.
  • Photo requirements are strict — White background, no glasses, specific dimensions. Many applications get rejected for incorrect photos.
  • Invitation letters matter — If visiting friends or family, their invitation letter should include their ID details, your relationship, and their address in China.
  • Keep copies of everything — Photocopy all documents and save digital copies on your phone or cloud storage.
  • Register at local police station — Within 24 hours of arrival, you must register at the local police station (hotels do this automatically).
  • Check if you qualify for visa-free entry — Some nationalities may enter without a visa for short stays. Always check the latest policies.

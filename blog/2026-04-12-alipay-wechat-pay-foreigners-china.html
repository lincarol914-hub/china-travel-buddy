How to Set Up Alipay and WeChat Pay as a Foreigner in China (2026 Guide)

April 12, 2026 8 min read China Travel Buddy

If you have been researching a trip to China, you have probably heard this warning: China is almost completely cashless, and foreign cards do not work at most places. That is largely true and it is the single biggest practical challenge for foreign visitors arriving in 2026.

The good news is that both Alipay and WeChat Pay now officially support international cards, and with some patience, foreigners can get fully set up before they even board the plane. This guide walks you through exactly how to do it, what to watch out for, and what to do when things go wrong.

Why Mobile Payments Are Non-Negotiable in China

China's payment ecosystem moved decisively to QR-code mobile payments over the past decade. In 2026, the vast majority of restaurants, supermarkets, convenience stores, street food vendors, taxis, museums, and even public toilets accept payment via Alipay or WeChat Pay and many only accept these. Visa, Mastercard, and international debit cards are rarely accepted outside of large international hotels.

Cash (Chinese yuan / RMB) remains a legal fallback, but getting cash requires either exchanging currency before arrival or finding an ATM that accepts foreign cards, a task that has become increasingly hit-or-miss. Setting up digital payments before your trip is simply the most reliable way to handle money in China.

Setting Up Alipay as a Foreigner - Step by Step

Alipay is operated by Ant Group (affiliated with Alibaba) and is the more foreigner-friendly of the two apps. It added international card support in 2023 and has continued to improve the foreign user experience since.

What You Need Before You Start

  • A smartphone (iOS or Android)
  • A valid international phone number (for SMS verification)
  • A Visa, Mastercard, or Maestro card issued outside China
  • Your passport (for identity verification)

Step-by-Step: Creating and Verifying Your Alipay Account

  1. 1
    Download the Alipay appSearch for "Alipay" in the App Store or Google Play. Download the international version with a blue icon, available in English.
  2. 2
    Sign up with your phone numberTap "Sign Up" and enter your international phone number including the country code. You will receive a 6-digit SMS code to verify.
  3. 3
    Complete identity verificationTap your profile icon, then Account and Security, then Identity Verification. Select "Non-mainland China resident" and enter your full name and passport number exactly as they appear on your passport.
  4. 4
    Link your international cardGo to Me, then Bank Cards, then Add Card. Enter your card number, expiry date, and CVV. Alipay supports Visa, Mastercard, and Maestro from most countries.
  5. 5
    Set a payment passwordCreate a 6-digit payment PIN required for every transaction.
  6. 6
    Test a small transactionScan a QR code for a small purchase to confirm everything works before your trip.
Pro Tip: Top Up Before You Arrive

While you can use your international card directly, some merchants prefer payments from a funded Alipay balance. Consider topping up with a small amount of 200 to 300 yuan before your trip to avoid any last-minute card declines.

What Alipay Can Do for You in China

Beyond paying at shops, Alipay is useful for booking train tickets via in-app mini-programs, paying for DiDi rides, ordering food delivery, topping up mobile data, and even accessing some government services. It is arguably the most useful app a foreigner can have on their phone in China.

Setting Up WeChat Pay as a Foreigner - Step by Step

WeChat Pay is embedded within the WeChat messaging app, China's dominant social platform with over 1.3 billion users. Setting up WeChat Pay is slightly more involved for foreigners, but it unlocks access to millions of QR code payment points across the country.

What You Need Before You Start

  • A WeChat account (or create one fresh)
  • A Visa or Mastercard issued outside China
  • Your phone number for verification
  • In some cases: a Chinese contact who can verify your account

Step-by-Step: Adding a Card to WeChat Pay

  1. 1
    Download WeChat and create an accountAvailable on App Store and Google Play. Sign up with your phone number and complete the basic profile setup.
  2. 2
    Navigate to WeChat PayIn the app, tap Me at the bottom right, then Services, then Wallet. This opens the WeChat Pay section.
  3. 3
    Add your international cardTap Bank Cards then Add Card. Enter your Visa or Mastercard details. WeChat will send a one-time password to your card's registered phone number to confirm.
  4. 4
    Set a payment PINCreate a 6-digit payment password separate from your WeChat account login password.
  5. 5
    Enable Scan to PayTap the QR code icon at the top of the WeChat home screen to access the payment scanner.
Important: New Account Verification

New WeChat accounts created outside China may be flagged for security verification. If prompted, you will need a friend with an existing WeChat account with at least 6 months of activity to scan your QR code and verify you.

Common Problems and How to Fix Them

  • Card declined when linking: Try a different card. Some banks block transactions routed through China. Contact your bank to whitelist international digital wallet charges, or use a travel-focused card like Wise, Revolut, or Monzo.
  • Identity verification fails: Double-check that your name and passport number are entered exactly as they appear on your passport. The verification process can take up to 24 hours.
  • OTP does not arrive: Check that your phone can receive international SMS. Try requesting the code again after 60 seconds.
  • Payment declined at merchant: Some merchants only accept one platform. Try switching apps. Check your payment limit as Alipay has a default daily limit for international cards of around 3,000 yuan.
  • New WeChat account blocked: You will need someone with an established account to scan your QR verification code. Many travelers ask in expat Facebook groups or ask at their hotel.
Do This Before You Leave Home

Do not wait until you arrive in China to set up these apps. Alipay's international card linking and identity verification works perfectly from outside China. Aim to have everything set up at least a week before departure and test with a small transaction.

What About Foreign Currency Exchange and ATMs?

Having some Chinese yuan in cash is still sensible as a backup. Exchange currency at your home country's bank before departure, or withdraw from ATMs at major Chinese airports on arrival. ICBC and Bank of China ATMs tend to accept foreign cards most reliably. However, do not rely on ATMs as your primary payment method as many smaller locations across China no longer have them.

Final Thoughts: Getting Ready to Pay Like a Local

Setting up Alipay and WeChat Pay is one of the most important steps you can take before visiting China in 2026. With both apps working on your phone, you will be able to pay for virtually anything from street dumplings to high-speed rail tickets without needing to fumble with cash or hunt for card terminals.

The setup process does take a bit of patience, especially for WeChat Pay, but it is entirely doable from home before your trip. Start at least a week before departure, and do not hesitate to get help if you hit a snag.
